# COMPSCI 426 — Summer 2026

Modern web applications are not single programs — they are distributed systems.
This course studies how real systems are built to handle millions of users: service
decomposition, asynchronous pipelines, caching strategies, replication, fault
tolerance, and load testing. You will read about how large-scale systems work, and
you will build one from scratch with your team.

### Course at a Glance

- **Credits:** 3
- **Format:** Lecture + team project (no exams)
- **Prerequisite:** COMPSCI 320 or COMPSCI 326 with a grade of C or better
- **Counts as:** Elective for CS and INFORM Majors

### What You Will Learn

The first part of the course covers the building blocks of scalable systems through
lectures and hands-on exercises:

- API gateways, load balancers, and reverse proxies
- Caching layers and cache invalidation strategies
- Asynchronous messaging with queues and pub/sub
- Background workers and pipeline processing
- Service replication and horizontal scaling
- Observability, health checks, and load testing with k6
- Fault tolerance: idempotency, poison pills, and dead letter queues

### The Project

The second half of the course is a four-sprint team project. Your team of 6–8
students will design and build a realistic distributed system using Docker Compose —
choosing from five different problem domains (event ticketing, food delivery, video
processing, IoT monitoring, or collaborative documents). You will prove your system
works under load with k6 tests and demo it to a TA at the end of each sprint.

# Final Grading Scheme

**COMPSCI 426 · Spring 2026**

This document explains how your final grade will be calculated and why the weights differ slightly from the original syllabus. The original plan included three unit tests that I was not able to create in time as the course was being developed in real time alongside our shared work. Rather than leave those weights in place or invent an exam at the end, I want to do this the honest way: redistribute the weights across the work you actually did, and give you a choice of three schemes so the final number reflects your strengths.

---

## What Changed, and Why

| Originally Planned | What Actually Ran |
| --- | --- |
| 10% Lecture Activities (up to 18) | 8 Lecture Activities (all delivered) |
| 30% Unit Tests (up to 3) | 0 Unit Tests (not delivered) |
| 30% Homework (up to 3) | 1 Homework - Reliable Campus Kiosk Simulation |
| 30% Project Assignments (up to 8) | 5 Project Assignments - Sprints 1–4 + Demo Day |
| 0% Exercises | |

The 30% originally allocated to unit tests has to go somewhere. The three variants below offer different ways to redistribute it across the work you already did or will do.

> **Guiding principles**
>
> - You will not be penalized for work I couldn't deliver.
> - Every variant sums to 100% and treats your completed work identically.
> - You will be given whichever of the three variants produces your best final grade.
> - No student has to do math to be treated fairly.

## Project Assignments - Internal Breakdown

Within the Project Assignments category, the individual sprints and Demo Day are weighted as follows. This internal split is the same across all three variants.

| Sprint | Focus | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Sprint 1** | Foundation | **10%** |
| **Sprint 2** | Async Pipelines and Caching | **15%** |
| **Sprint 3** | Reliability and Poison Pills | **15%** |
| **Sprint 4** | Replication, Scaling, and Polish | **20%** |
| **Demo Day** | Final expo | **10%** |
| **Total** | | **70%** |

The 70% total is the share of the Project Assignments category itself. What that category is worth within your overall grade depends on which variant applies - see below.

## The Three Variants

You don't have to pick - the variant that produces your best grade will be applied automatically at the end of the semester.

### Variant A - Neutral Redistribution

*The safe, proportional default.*

| Category | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Lecture Activities (8 activities) | **14%** |
| Homework (Homework 1) | **43%** |
| Project Assignments (Sprints + Demo) | **43%** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Best for** - students who performed consistently across all three categories.

### Variant B - Project-Weighted

*Lets the sprints and Demo Day drive the grade.*

| Category | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Lecture Activities (8 activities) | **15%** |
| Homework (Homework 1) | **25%** |
| Project Assignments (Sprints + Demo) | **60%** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Best for** - students whose homework grade is weaker than their project trajectory, or who want end-of-semester work to have the most influence.

### Variant C - Homework and Activities Weighted

*Locks in strong early-semester performance.*

| Category | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Lecture Activities (8 activities) | **20%** |
| Homework (Homework 1) | **40%** |
| Project Assignments (Sprints + Demo) | **40%** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Best for** - students who did well on the lecture activities and Homework 1 and want that work to carry more weight.

## How This Will Work

- **Automatic best-of-three.** At the end of the semester I will compute your final grade under all three variants and apply whichever produces the best letter grade for you. You do not need to pick a variant or submit a request.
- **No penalty for missing work I didn't assign.** Only the work that was actually assigned counts. Homeworks 2 and 3, the unit tests, and the additional project assignments are simply not part of the calculation.
- **Your completed grades don't change.** What you earned on Lecture Activities, Homework 1, and the sprints is what you earned. The variants only change how those scores are weighted in the final average.
- **Focus on the work ahead.** Sprints 2–4 and Demo Day remain as planned. You can see from the variants that strong work there has a real impact on your final grade under B, and a meaningful one under A and C.

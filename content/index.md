Welcome to **COMPSCI 426 Scalable Systems**.

Modern web applications look seamless from the outside, but behind every fast, reliable interface is a complex system of services, data stores, infrastructure, and engineering decisions working together under pressure. In this course, you will study how real-world systems scale, why they become difficult to design and maintain, and how architecture, performance, reliability, security, and teamwork shape the software people depend on every day. If you want to understand what it takes to build systems that hold up under real usage, this course is designed for you.

A more detailed outline of the course policies and requirements are maintained in the [[Syllabus|syllabus]]. You are expected to read this completely and understand our expectations.

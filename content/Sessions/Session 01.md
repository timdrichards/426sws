## What is a Session?
A session is what you are looking at right now. It is typically aligned with what is traditionally referred to as a lecture. We deviate from the term *lecture* to free us from the image of a person standing in front of you "lecturing". There are times when it is helpful to deliver content in that form, but there are other ways that can be more effective. The term session transcends the lecture definition into something more flexible and dynamic. In fact, you may be seeing this for the first time during a lecture-format session or perhaps you are reading this as part of an online version of this course. Either way, the delivery of this information and knowledge is getting to you in a way that hopefully allows you to learn that topics we are about to explore.

## What is the Syllabus?
Given that this is a course in an academic setting as part of an academic institution, we traditionally provide a contract upon which you agree to as part of being enrolled in a course. This is typically referred to as a [[Syllabus]] and it is absolutely something that you should read. It provides all sorts of useful information including expectations, workload, assignments, and grading criteria. You must use this as your roadmap to the course and its operations as well as its expectations and requirements from you.

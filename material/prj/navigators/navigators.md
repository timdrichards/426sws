## Communication

**All communication with teams happens over email.** Do not use Teams, Discord, Slack, or any other channel. Email creates a clear record and is the professional standard for this course.

When a team emails you, reply promptly — within 24 hours on weekdays. If you need to escalate something to the instructor, forward the email thread rather than summarizing it verbally.

Keep the instructor (Richards) cc'd on any email where you are communicating a grading decision or a deadline accommodation. For routine scheduling emails, cc is not necessary.

## Scheduling Sprint Demos

Each sprint has a two-day demo window after the submission deadline. You are responsible for scheduling a 30-minute demo slot with each of your assigned teams within that window.

| Sprint | Demo window    | Submission deadline |
| ------ | -------------- | ------------------- |
| 1      | 04.14 – 04.16  | 04.14 before class  |
| 2      | 04.21 – 04.23  | 04.21 before class  |
| 3      | 04.28 – 04.30  | 04.28 before class  |
| 4      | Demo Day 05.07 | 05.05 before class  |

**Book slots in advance.** My recommendation is to book all three sprints for your teams before the first demo window opens. This allows teams to plan ahead and avoids the risk of scheduling conflicts as the windows get closer. The most important one to schedule NOW is Sprint 1, since that demo window is next week.

### Outreach Email Template

Send one email per team. Fill in the bracketed fields. Use the team's Canvas group email or the email addresses of the team members you have on file.

---

**Subject:** COMPSCI 426 — Sprint [N] Demo Scheduling

Hi [Team Name],

I'm [your name], your navigator for the project. I'll be running your sprint demos throughout the project.

Your Sprint [N] submission is due before class on [date]. Once you've pushed your code and tagged `sprint-[N]` on your `main` branch, please reply to this email with **two or three times that work for a 30-minute demo** between [demo window start] and [demo window end]. I'll confirm a slot by end of day.

A few things to have ready for the demo:

- Your `sprint-[N]` tag pushed to `main` on GitHub
- `docker compose up` starting cleanly from a fresh clone of that tag
- Your sprint report committed to `main` at the tagged commit

I'll clone your repo fresh at the start of the demo, so make sure everything works from a clean checkout before we meet.

Let me know if you have any questions.

[Your name]
COMPSCI 426 Teaching Staff

---

You do not need to send a separate email when the demo is confirmed — a short reply with the agreed time is sufficient. Add the slot to your own calendar, a calendar invite to the team, and send a one-line confirmation to the team asking them to confirm the calendar invite. If you notice that a team member hasn't accepted the invite after a day, follow up with them directly to confirm they are aware of the demo indicating who hasn't accepted.

### Before Each Demo

Before the team arrives (or before joining the call), do the following:

1. Confirm the sprint tag exists on `main`: `git log --oneline sprint-[N] -1`
2. Confirm the sprint report is present at the tag: check `sprint-reports/SPRINT-[N].md`
3. Clone the repo fresh into a temporary directory — do not use a local copy you've been working from
4. Run `docker compose up --build` and verify the system starts before the team is present

If the tag is missing or the system does not start from the fresh clone, note it immediately. Do not let the team debug during the demo slot — the submission is what it is at the tag.

### Individual and Repository Grades

In addition to the sprint rubric, two components are assessed across the full project lifecycle:

**Individual grade (25% of project grade):** Verified using `git log --author` on each tagged commit, cross-referenced against the ownership claims in each team member's sprint report. Look for commits in the directories each person claimed. Commits that only appear as merge commits, or that touch files outside a person's stated scope, are red flags. Peer evaluations (collected at Demo Day) also feed into this grade.

**Repository grade (15% of project grade):** Assessed once at the `sprint-4` tag. Covers README quality, repository structure, commit message hygiene, sprint tags, `.gitignore`, and Docker Compose organization. The [Repository Structure](/spring-2026/prj/repository/) document describes the standards in detail.

### Canvas Score Entry

The exact process for entering scores into Canvas has not been finalized. Do not enter grades until you receive instructions to do so. After each demo, record your scores and notes locally (a shared sheet or github repo will be provided) and hold them until the entry process is confirmed.
